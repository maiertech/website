## Transcript

- Tech jobs with visa sponsorship are rather rare.
- I assume that you are already located in the Netherlands:
  - You need to have very specialized skills to get a visa sponsorship.
  - Or your employer is desperate because they have a bad working climate.
- Job tiers:
  - Fully remote international
  - Fully remote local (very rare)
  - Hybrid local (most common, often Dutch speaking)
  - On-site local (up and coming, almost always Dutch speaking)
- Salary ranges (no figures):
  - Big Tech international: absurdly high because it's not tailored to the Dutch tax system.
  - Bigger tech Amsterdam
  - Government and semi-government
  - Midfield tech (non-tech companies with a CAO)
  - Local startups
  - Agencies
  - Mom-and-Pop
- Geographic distribution:
  - Amsterdam
  - Utrecht
  - Eindhoven (lots of tech jobs but not many frontend)
  - Den Haag (non-profit and government)
  - Rotterdam (very few tech jobs)
  - Random places in the middle of nowhere
- Freelance:
  - Wet DBA scare companies
  - It's fine to freelance as long as you are strictly task-based.
  - Cross-border fulltime employment via freelance is a grayzone.
- Cross-border employment:
  - Even within the EU you need an EoR.
  - I wonder what contracts people have with an EoR.
- Job titles:
  - A product engineer has a different meaning in the local job market.
- Websites:
  - Indeed: narrow down geographically
  - LinkedIn: ghost jobs and easy apply put you up for a failure. Try to find the job on the company
    website and apply there.
  - Google search
  - local job boards
  - Communities, Discord
